# Have a Sip ☕

A personal engineering blog inspired by quiet café conversations.

Instead of teaching as an expert, the blog shares the journey of learning software engineering one small sip at a time.

---

# Vision

Create a calm place for thoughtful engineering writing.

The goal is not to maximize engagement, comments, or social interactions.

The goal is to document learning, share experiences, and occasionally invite readers into a conversation.

---

# Product Philosophy

Reading comes first.

Conversation comes second.

Writing is the core experience.

Comments exist to support the writing, not replace it.

---

# The Table

The Table is the comment section.

However, it should not feel like a traditional comment section.

It should feel like a quiet conversation after the article ends.

---

# Optional Per Post

Not every article has a table.

Some posts:

- allow discussion

Some posts:

- are read-only

Readers are not explicitly notified when discussion is unavailable.

If a table exists, it appears naturally at the end of the article.

If not, the article simply ends.

---

# Take a Sip

The Table remains hidden initially.

At the end of an article:

```text
☕ The Table

4 thoughts shared tonight.

[ Take a Sip ]
```

Comments are hidden.

When the reader chooses to participate:

1. Click Take a Sip
2. Join the table
3. Conversation becomes visible
4. Reader may leave a thought

---

# Reader Experience

Default behavior:

Read article.

Conversation is optional.

Readers are never encouraged to comment.

No engagement-driven prompts.

No "Leave a comment below."

No social pressure.

---

# Comment Philosophy

Comments are secondary.

Success is not measured by:

- comment count
- engagement
- reactions

Success is measured by:

- thoughtful writing
- meaningful reading
- authentic learning

- followers

Participation should feel lightweight.

The "cup" concept acts as a ritual for joining the table.

Not a permanent identity.

Not a social profile.
